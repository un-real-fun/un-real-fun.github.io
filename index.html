30-50 % of all economic activity in the world constitute of non-capitalist economic activities (Gibson-Graham, 2008). Yet in the capitalist, neoliberalist machine we are living in today, monetized economies are by far the ones dominating not only the discourse, but our sense of reality. We want to shift our sight to see the unseen, to the non-market transactions, unpaid household work, alternative currencies, co-op exchange, hunting and gathering. What are the diverse economies that exists today and what could they be in the future? What is the definition of value, and how is it (and could) be determined, felt, understood, recorded and transferred.

The workshop will explore the theme by using improvisation and role-play as tools for creating design fiction(s). Design Fictions are traditionally meant to be consumed, a story to be read, watched or experienced - they are a way to raise concerns and dialogue about certain topics and potential futures. Through performative tools we hope to break this distinction between knowledge producers and the audience.

The workshop will also have play and fun as a central objective, as a contrast to how design today is engulfed in the neoliberalist engine - constantly forced to be more effective and productive. The participants will play and perform with new sensibilities on the heterogeneity in economics.
